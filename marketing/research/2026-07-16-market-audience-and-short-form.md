# Nepal, Hindu astrology, app category, and short-form market research

**Research date:** 2026-07-16  
**Latest Nepal digital data period:** predominantly October 2025  
**Method:** primary government/platform pages and live app-store listings accessed through the
repository-mandated gstack browser.

## Decisions this research supports

1. Keep parents and grandparents aged 40–80 as the product's core user, but test younger adult
   children and diaspora family members as distinct discoverer/installer roles.
2. Produce for Instagram and TikTok, but test Facebook Reels early because its reachable Nepal
   audience is much broader than Instagram's.
3. Use an 8-second Veo output as source footage. Show the proposition and genuine app UI within
   roughly three seconds rather than playing the full generated scene first.
4. Lead with family, Nepali cultural utility, real calculation evidence, and calm accessibility.
   Do not compete on fear, exact predictions, consultation urgency, or feature volume.
5. Treat TikTok organic publishing and TikTok paid advertising as separate compliance decisions.
   Paid horoscope/fortune-telling eligibility for Nepal is not confirmed by the current policy.

## Nepal digital opportunity

[DataReportal Digital 2026: Nepal](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2026-nepal)
reports a population of 29.6 million, median age 25.3, 16.6 million internet users, and 14.8
million social-media identities. Platform advertising tools indicated potential reach of 14.8
million on Facebook, 4.35 million on Instagram, and 11.0 million on Messenger.

| Signal | Latest reported figure | Planning implication |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Population | 29.6M | Meaningful culturally focused home market |
| Median age | 25.3 | Short-form discovery and adult-child installer creative deserve testing |
| Ages 18–24 | 13.6% (~4.0M) | Adult identity/learning exploratory segment |
| Ages 25–34 | 16.7% (~4.9M) | Relationships, career, migration, family-enabler segment |
| Ages 35–44 | 12.6% (~3.7M) | Household planning, family, Patro, Muhurat segment |
| Internet users | 16.6M / 56.0% | Keep assets efficient and onboarding resilient to mixed connectivity |
| Social identities | 14.8M | Large discovery surface; not a count of unique people |
| Facebook potential ad reach | 14.8M | Primary test channel for older product users and family sharing |
| Instagram potential ad reach | 4.35M | Relevant but not representative of all Nepal adults |
| Rural population | ~77% | Avoid Kathmandu-only settings and assumptions |

DataReportal does not publish a Nepal TikTok reach figure in this report. We will not invent one.
Advertising reach is neither monthly active users nor a deduplicated population count, and age
group population is not the same as reachable app demand.

## Hindu and diaspora context

Nepal's National Statistics Office counted 23,677,744 Hindus, or 81.2% of the population, in the
2021 census. The official analysis is available as [Religions in Nepal](https://nsonepal.gov.np/content/13373/religions-in-nepal/).
This supports a culturally Hindu product context; it does not justify assuming that every Hindu
uses or believes in Jyotish.

[Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/2025/06/09/how-the-global-religious-landscape-changed-from-2010-to-2020/)
estimated 1.2 billion Hindus worldwide in 2020, with about 99% in Asia-Pacific and 95% in India.
The broader Hindu opportunity is therefore not one homogeneous global audience. India expansion
would require its own language, calendar, terminology, regional, policy, and product tests.

The Nepal census analysis [Adolescents and Youth in Nepal](https://nsonepal.gov.np/content/13374/adolescents-and-youth-in-nepal/)
recorded 2,190,592 Nepalis abroad in 2021. Destination percentages published for the younger
10–24 cohort must not be generalized to all diaspora adults. The useful creative hypothesis is
qualitative: Patro, family profiles, bilingual explanation, and shared cultural time can connect
people living away from home without using guilt.

## Category and competitor evidence

The category has large observed distribution without relying on speculative market forecasts:

| Product | Live store signal | Model and lesson |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AstroTalk | 100M+ Google Play installs; ~1.59M reviews | Human consultation marketplace; proves demand but visible reviews include billing anxiety |
| AstroSage Kundli | 50M+ installs; ~1.06M reviews | Deep Vedic utility and AI breadth; visible reviews include ads, rate clarity, and answer-consistency concerns |
| Hamro Patro | 10M+ installs; ~152K reviews | Nepal super-app with Patro, news, communication, Rashifal, Kundali, and consultation |
| Nepali Patro | 10M+ installs; ~70K reviews | Strong calendar/cultural-root identity across generations and geographies |
| Co–Star | ~205K US App Store ratings at access | Western identity/friendship/social-graph astrology |
| CHANI | ~55.6K US App Store ratings | Trusted wellness/audio/ritual subscription positioning |
| Nebula | ~170K US App Store ratings | Broad spiritual guidance, compatibility, and psychic chat |

Install counts are Google Play buckets, ratings/review counts change, and App Store evidence is
storefront-specific. Review weaknesses are themes from a small visible sample, not a formal
sentiment study.

Three proven category models recur: free calculation leading to consultation, subscription
content, and identity/relationship sharing. Jyotish Baje's defensible combination is different:

- one household with multiple Kundalis and relationship context;
- real traditional calculations with explainable evidence;
- Nepali/English, Patro, readable design, and voice;
- a calm companion rather than a consultation timer or everything portal.

## What short-form platforms currently reward

[TikTok's official creative guidance](https://ads.us.tiktok.com/help/article/creative-best-practices?lang=en)
recommends vertical 9:16, sound, people/creator-style presentation, a native rather than overly
polished feel, the proposition within three seconds, the hook within six seconds, captions, a
clear CTA, and multiple meaningfully different creatives. Its current
[video specifications](https://ads.us.tiktok.com/help/article/video-ads-specifications?lang=en)
set technical requirements that must be rechecked at export time.

[Meta's Instagram Reels guide](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads-guide/update/video/instagram-reels)
recommends fullscreen 9:16 video, captions, sound, and UI-safe composition. It identifies large
top/bottom exclusion zones; every export should still be previewed in the live upload interface
because platform chrome changes.

### Recommended edit grammar

For an 8–12 second final treatment:

| Time | Purpose |
| ---: | --- |
| 0.0–1.5s | strongest slice of the Veo pattern interrupt |
| 1.5–3.0s | benefit/question and transition into real UI |
| 3.0–8.0s | genuine app interaction and visible result |
| 8.0–12.0s | trust cue and one CTA |

For 15–18 seconds, intercut a second part of the generated source around a longer UI proof. The
Veo clip is raw material, not the finished ad.

## Policy and ethical constraints

[TikTok's misleading-content policy](https://ads.us.tiktok.com/help/article/tiktok-ads-policy-misleading-and-false-content?lang=en)
prohibits exaggerated/absolute outcomes and requires disclosure for materially AI-generated ad
content. Veo dramatizations should use the platform's AIGC label or clear disclosure even when a
specific placement does not force it.

TikTok's current [other products and services policy](https://ads.us.tiktok.com/help/article/tiktok-ads-policy-other-products-and-services?lang=en)
restricts horoscope/fortune-telling advertising by market and prohibits exact predictions,
guaranteed outcomes, or definitive solutions. Nepal and India are not identified as allowed
markets on the accessed policy page. Confirm eligibility directly before paid spend. UAE, Qatar,
and Saudi Arabia are explicitly prohibited for this category; the US, Canada, UK, and Australia
are listed as allowed subject to conditions.

[Meta Advertising Standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/)
prohibit deceptive offers and ads that assert or imply personal attributes such as religion,
beliefs, age, health, or vulnerable financial status. Creative may invite people interested in
Nepali/Jyotish content; it must not tell the viewer that the advertiser knows their religion,
marital problem, health status, or fate.

## Initial test portfolio

- Nepal 25–34: family-enabler, relationship evidence, career/timing, Nepali vs bilingual.
- Nepal 35–44: household profiles, Patro, Muhurat, family planning.
- Nepal 40+: Facebook Reels with readable Nepali, voice, and family utility.
- Adult diaspora 25–44: cultural continuity, shared family context, Patro.
- Nepal 18–24: limited adult exploration around learning and first Kundali; do not let cheap
  attention displace activated-household quality.

Start with substantially different concepts, then confirm winners by changing one factor at a
time. Attention metrics diagnose the opening; activation and retention decide whether the ad
helped the business.
