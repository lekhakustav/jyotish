# Short-form creative swipe file: astrology and Nepali app examples

**Research date:** 2026-07-16
**Method:** public pages inspected with the repository-mandated gstack browser. YouTube player
metadata supplied dates, durations, views, and likes. Selected videos were also inspected at
multiple timecodes and through YouTube storyboard frames.

## What this evidence can and cannot tell us

This is an observational swipe file, not a leaderboard. Public views and likes are useful
creative signals, but they do not reveal retention, shares, paid delivery, spend, installs,
subscriptions, or profit. Publication date, distribution, audience size, topic, and algorithmic
exposure differ between examples. Like-to-view figures below are simple public-signal ratios,
not engagement rates or causal performance estimates.

The accessible sample is strongest for official AstroTalk YouTube Shorts and includes one highly
relevant official Nepali Patro product Short. Meta Ad Library and TikTok Creative Center both
returned HTTP 403 from the required headless research environment, so no claims about current
Meta or TikTok ad winners are made here.

## Testable creative patterns

Pre-register these as hypotheses. Change one named factor at a time after broader concept tests.

1. **Result before navigation:** notification/result by 2s versus app navigation first. Hold the
   same voiceover, UI task, CTA, audience, and posting window.
2. **Real UI by 3s:** transition from Veo source footage into genuine app capture by 2.5–3.0s
   versus by 5.0–6.0s. Primary diagnostic: three-second hold; business outcome: activated household.
3. **Dated relevance:** a real upcoming Patro/transit date versus evergreen wording. Never imply
   harm or inevitable outcomes.
4. **Numbered promise:** `3 things this screen explains` versus a non-numbered question, with the
   same information and length.
5. **Host trust cue:** Nepali expert/family presenter for 2–3s versus voiceover over the same
   visual proof. Do not fabricate credentials.
6. **Visual refresh cadence:** host/UI/B-roll changes every 2–4s versus a single continuous shot.
   Keep total duration and script constant.
7. **Persistent headline:** short safe-zone headline throughout versus first-two-seconds only.
   Keep subtitles in both versions.
8. **Language treatment:** natural Nepali versus Nepali voice with concise English feature terms.
   Test within the same audience rather than treating language as a proxy for age or education.
9. **CTA specificity:** `Try this with your family chart` versus `See how it works`; both must lead
   to the exact demonstrated screen.
10. **Serial family:** a repeated neutral identity/utility series versus unrelated one-offs. Use a
    stable template while issuing a new `creative_id` for every material treatment.

## Recommended 8–12 second assembly

The observed competitor Shorts range from 27–81 seconds, except the 12-second Nepali Patro
product demonstration. Therefore the 8-second Veo render should be source footage, not the whole
advertisement:

| Time | Edit function |
| ---: | --- |
| 0.0–1.5s | One visually clear Veo pattern interrupt plus an honest question or utility promise |
| 1.5–3.0s | Payoff preview, notification, calculation result, or host trust cue |
| 3.0–8.5s | Genuine app capture proving the exact action and result |
| 8.5–12.0s | Brand, one CTA, and AI-generated-content disclosure where required |

Use burn-in captions, keep essential text inside current platform safe zones, and make the first
frame understandable without sound. Competitor caution, fear, hidden-secret, and remedy language
is recorded as market observation only. Jyotish Baje creative must not use fear, inevitability,
guaranteed money/marriage/health outcomes, caste discrimination, or pressure to buy remedies.
