# Channel and distribution plan

## Channel roles

| Channel | Primary job | Initial audience hypothesis | Use now |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Instagram Reels | discovery, saves, shares, visual proof | 24–44, enablers, couples, diaspora | organic; paid after launch gates |
| TikTok | rapid hook learning and creator-native storytelling | 18–39 adult enablers and diaspora | organic; verify ad-market availability before paid spend |
| Facebook Reels/feed | reach the documented older product audience and family sharers | 40+ household guides and elders | strong test priority |
| YouTube Shorts | searchable evergreen proof and cross-platform learning | broad adult/diaspora | repurpose selectively |
| App stores | conversion proof, reviews, localized screenshots | every installer | required destination before paid scale |

Instagram and TikTok are production priorities because the requested format is short vertical
video. They should not become an artificial constraint that prevents testing Facebook, where
Nepal's reachable adult audience is materially broader.

## Organic-first launch

Paid acquisition remains gated by privacy-policy reconciliation, store links, and attribution
design. Organic publication can establish creative baselines while those are resolved, provided
claims and synthetic-data rules pass review.

### Weekly mix

- 2 family or diaspora stories with app proof.
- 1 precise traditional explainer (Kundali, Dasha, Panchang, Muhurat).
- 1 interaction prompt that elicits real questions without offering a prediction in comments.
- 1 accessibility/cultural-identity demonstration (Nepali, voice, Patro).
- 1 fast iteration of the best recent hook with only one deliberate change.

Cadence is a starting constraint, not a growth law. Maintain quality and lineage rather than
uploading filler to satisfy a quota.

## First 30 published creatives

Use a balanced portfolio rather than thirty unrelated ideas:

- 6 hook families × 2 audience roles × at least 2 executions.
- Preserve one control treatment long enough to estimate normal variance.
- Reuse the same app-proof clip when testing opening emotion, or reuse the same opening when
  testing proof feature.
- Tag every factor so the analysis can pool evidence by hook, voice, speaker, proof, and CTA.

## Publication mechanics

- Export a clean 1080×1920 master without platform watermark.
- Upload natively to each platform; create a separate `publication_id` for every upload.
- Use the platform's licensed music library when music rights do not travel across platforms.
- Write platform-native captions, but keep the creative treatment constant when the post is part
  of a controlled cross-platform diagnostic.
- Reply to genuine product questions; never provide high-stakes personalized predictions in
  public comments.

## Paid test sequence after gates are cleared

1. Run a low-budget A/A test to validate duplicate delivery, exports, joins, and app funnel.
2. Run randomized platform-native split tests with one primary metric.
3. Optimize first for cost per activated household, not cheapest click or raw view.
4. Separate prospecting from retargeting and Nepal from each diaspora country.
5. Scale only after activation and early retention agree with platform attention signals.

## Viral definition

“Viral” is an observed outlier, not a target promise. Define it relative to the account's own
history: unusually high reach velocity plus top-decile or top-five-percent share/save rate while
qualified app actions do not collapse. A high-view post that attracts the wrong audience is a
creative observation, not business success.
