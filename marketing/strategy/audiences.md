# Audience hypotheses

These are testable acquisition hypotheses. The 40–80 product audience comes from the product
vision; all installer/channel mappings must be validated with campaign and activation data.

## Role 1: household guide, 40–54

- **Context:** parent managing children, relationships, rituals, dates, and household decisions.
- **Question:** “What does this period mean for my child or family, and what should we do?”
- **Proof:** Parivar profiles, Dasha, relationships, Muhurat, personalized Pandit answer.
- **Emotional register:** care, preparedness, reassurance without certainty.
- **Likely channels:** Facebook/Reels first; Instagram as a test; family shares and Messenger.
- **Activation signal:** self profile plus one family member and one completed personalized report.

## Role 2: family elder, 55–80

- **Context:** regular Patro/Rashifal user who values readable Nepali and familiar concepts.
- **Question:** “Can I see today's tithi and ask in my own language without navigating a maze?”
- **Proof:** Nepali UI, large type, voice input, Patro, Rashifal, direct Pandit entry.
- **Emotional register:** respect, familiarity, calm utility.
- **Likely channels:** Facebook/Reels and family introduction; do not assume TikTok/Instagram
  self-discovery is the primary path.
- **Activation signal:** Nepali language selected, birth profile saved, repeat weekly guidance.

## Role 3: family enabler, 25–39 in Nepal

- **Context:** digitally fluent adult child who discovers tools for parents or for family use.
- **Question:** “Is there finally a clean Nepali app I can set up for my parents and also use?”
- **Proof:** fast onboarding/reveal, bilingual switch, family profile addition, shareable Parivar QR.
- **Emotional register:** pride, usefulness, intergenerational care.
- **Likely channels:** Instagram Reels and TikTok; test Facebook/YouTube Shorts cross-posts.
- **Activation signal:** self profile plus another family profile or QR action.

## Role 4: diaspora connector, 25–44

- **Context:** lives away from Nepal and wants cultural continuity and easier family conversation.
- **Question:** “How do I stay connected to Nepali dates and understand what my family is asking?”
- **Proof:** Patro, Nepali/English, family profiles, remote QR exchange, festival/tithi context.
- **Emotional register:** belonging, distance, continuity; avoid guilt manipulation.
- **Likely channels:** Instagram/TikTok by country and language; family sharing.
- **Activation signal:** non-Nepal market, Patro or family feature use, D7 return.

## Role 5: couple or marriage decision participant, 24–39

- **Context:** curious about Kundli matching but resistant to a single fatalistic score.
- **Question:** “What does the traditional match actually say, and where should we talk more?”
- **Proof:** Ashtakoota evidence, Mangal context, strengths/tensions, practical conversation topics.
- **Emotional register:** curiosity and clarity; never judgment or coercion.
- **Likely channels:** Instagram/TikTok.
- **Activation signal:** second profile added and a completed relationship or matching report.

## Segmentation rules

- Keep `user_role` and `installer_role` separate.
- Keep Nepal and diaspora markets separate; language is not a proxy for location.
- Report platform-native age buckets; never pretend combined buckets precisely measure age 40–80.
- Do not target minors. The app is not directed at children under 13, and sensitive birth/family
  data warrants adult-oriented creative and consent.
- Treat gender, caste, relationship, and fertility inferences as sensitive. Do not create targeting
  or creative that exploits them.
