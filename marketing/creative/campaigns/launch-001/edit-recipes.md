# Second-by-second edit recipes

## Shared edit specification

- Canvas: 1080 × 1920, 9:16, 30 fps delivery. Preserve the original Veo frame rate in the
  source bin; conform once on the timeline.
- Duration: 16.5–17.0 seconds. All concepts use an 8-second Veo source render, but only 2–4
  seconds of it should appear in the finished creative.
- Product speed: state the proposition inside the first 3 seconds and make genuine app UI
  visible by 2.2–2.8 seconds. Never run the full 8-second generated shot before proof.
- Conservative overlay-safe box: keep hook, captions, disclaimers, and critical labels outside
  the top 14%, bottom 35%, and side 6%. At 1080 × 1920 this means keep critical overlay text
  approximately inside x=65–1015 and y=269–1248. Platform controls can still cover app pixels,
  so reframe the important proof into the center rather than relying on the bottom navigation.
- Captions: burned in, sentence case, two lines maximum, high contrast, 3–7 words per card.
  Highlight only one word per card in saffron. Do not imitate app UI typography.
- Generated footage: mark as AI-generated in the editor metadata and apply the platform's AIGC
  disclosure/label at publication. Never call generated people users, customers, or a real event.
- App footage: no zoom beyond 115% unless captured above delivery resolution. Use an editorial
  saffron hairline or 110% focus crop to direct attention; never fabricate a tap, result, graph,
  notification, score, or app screen.
- Sound: voice peaks near -6 dBFS; music under speech around -24 to -18 LUFS integrated as a
  starting range; no temple bells, ominous drones, heartbeat, or magical whooshes. Use a short
  tactile cut sound at the first app reveal.
- End card: real approved app icon/wordmark, one CTA, 2.2 seconds minimum. Until a verified store
  URL exists, use `Meet Jyotish Baje` rather than `Download now`.
- Every hook, voice, caption, language, CTA, music, proof path, or timing change creates a new
  `creative_id`.

The on-screen copy below is the English master. Localize—not transliterate—the hook and labels
for Nepali treatments, and have every Nepali frame reviewed in the final exported video.
