# App Review notes (Apple App Review / Google Play pre-launch notes)

Hello App Review Team,

Nepali Hindu families regularly use and exchange Kundlis when planning marriages, ceremonies,
festivals and other important family events. However, existing apps do not provide a simple,
private way for Nepali families to share these religious records with one another. Jyotish Baje
addresses this specific unmet need through private, family-to-family Kundli sharing.

Users can share a Nepal-specific Hindu Kundli through a private Kundli QR code. The recipient
scans it, selects their family relationship and adds the Kundli to their household for
relationship-aware guidance — without repeatedly entering sensitive birth information.

The app is also built around Nepal's Bikram Sambat calendar, Nepali Hindu festivals, Tithis,
rituals and religious traditions. Kundlis become connected family records that can support
ceremonies, marriages and household planning across generations.

To our knowledge, no other App Store app combines Nepal-specific Hindu Kundlis, Nepali festivals
and private family-to-family Kundli sharing in this way. Jyotish Baje is therefore a culturally
specific social utility for trusted Nepali Hindu families — not another generic horoscope app.

The app opens directly to **My Kundli & QR**, and the previous Home experience is now a separate
**Religious** tab containing Nepal-specific Bikram Sambat dates, Tithi, festivals, Temple of the
Day, Rashifal, Patro and traditional guidance. This hierarchy makes the private Kundli exchange
the primary product workflow.

Suggested review path:

1. Open **My Kundli & QR**. This is the default first tab.
2. Tap **Share My Kundli** to view the private QR and the exact disclosure of what it contains.
3. From another device or the paste fallback, open **Scan Kundli QR**.
4. Scan the code, choose a relationship and add the profile.
5. Open a saved person to view the Kundli generated from the shared birth profile.
6. Open **Religious** to view Nepal-specific religious dates and guidance as supporting context.

Privacy boundary: the v1 QR includes the sharer's name and birth details. It does not include a
calculated Kundli, relationship label, chat history, account credentials or analytics identifier.
The app does not claim that the exchange creates a permanent mutual social connection.

Sign-in and demo account: the app requires an account (Sign in with Apple, Google, or email and
password). A working demo account **must** be entered in App Review Information before
submission:

- Email: `review.test+appstore@sodhera.com`
- Password: `<held by owner — do not commit; see submission checklist>`

Permissions: camera is requested only when the user opens **Scan Kundli QR** and is used solely
for live QR detection — no photo or video is captured or stored. Microphone and speech
recognition are requested only when the user taps the voice-input button.

We respectfully ask that this distinctive experience be reconsidered under Guideline 4.3(b).
